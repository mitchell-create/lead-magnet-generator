# Prospeo Seniority Levels - Official List

## Prospeo Supported Seniority Values

These are the exact seniority options available in Prospeo (from Prospeo dashboard):

### Executive & Ownership Level
1. **`Founder/Owner`** - Company founder or business owner
2. **`C-Suite`** - C-level executives (CEO, CFO, CTO, COO, CMO, etc.)
3. **`Partner`** - Business partner (common in law firms, consultancies, partnerships)

### Leadership & Management Level
4. **`Vice President`** - VP level executives
5. **`Head`** - Head of department/function
6. **`Director`** - Director level

### Operational Level
7. **`Manager`** - Manager level
8. **`Senior`** - Senior individual contributor

### Entry Level
9. **`Intern`** - Intern positions
10. **`Entry`** - Entry-level positions

---

## What Each Level Means

### **Founder/Owner**
- **Definition:** Company founder or business owner
- **Decision Power:** Complete - owns or co-owns the company
- **Scope:** Company-wide vision and strategy
- **Budget Authority:** Full company budget control
- **Best For:** Companies targeting founders/entrepreneurs, early-stage partnerships

---

### **C-Suite**
- **Definition:** Top-level executives (CEO, CFO, CTO, COO, CMO, Chief [X] Officer)
- **Decision Power:** Very high - company-wide strategic decisions
- **Scope:** Major divisions or entire company
- **Budget Authority:** Large budgets, P&L responsibility
- **Best For:** Enterprise sales, strategic partnerships, major purchases
- **Common Titles:** CEO, CFO, CTO, COO, CMO, CHRO, CSO

---

### **Partner**
- **Definition:** Business partner (often in law firms, consultancies, partnerships)
- **Decision Power:** High - shared ownership and decision-making
- **Scope:** Business partnership level decisions
- **Budget Authority:** Significant budget control (shared)
- **Best For:** Professional services partnerships, consultancies

---

### **Vice President (VP)**
- **Definition:** VP level executives, senior leadership
- **Decision Power:** High - sets strategy for their area
- **Scope:** Major departments or product lines
- **Budget Authority:** Significant budgets, often P&L responsibility
- **Best For:** Department-level strategic decisions, large purchases, vendor selection
- **Common Titles:** VP, SVP, EVP, Vice President

---

### **Head**
- **Definition:** Head of department, function, or area
- **Decision Power:** Medium-High - strategic decisions in their function
- **Scope:** Functional area (e.g., Head of Sales, Head of Marketing)
- **Budget Authority:** Department budgets
- **Best For:** Department-level decisions, functional leadership targeting
- **Common Titles:** Head of [Department], Head of [Function]

---

### **Director**
- **Definition:** Director level, oversees multiple teams or functional areas
- **Decision Power:** Medium-High - strategic decisions in their function
- **Scope:** Functional area or multiple teams
- **Budget Authority:** Department budgets
- **Best For:** Department-level decisions, vendor selection, strategic initiatives
- **Common Titles:** Director, Senior Director, Executive Director

---

### **Manager**
- **Definition:** Manager level, manages teams and operations
- **Decision Power:** Medium - tactical and some strategic decisions
- **Scope:** Team or small department
- **Budget Authority:** Team budgets
- **Best For:** Operational decisions, smaller purchases, team-level initiatives
- **Common Titles:** Manager, Senior Manager, General Manager, Team Manager

---

### **Senior**
- **Definition:** Senior individual contributor (non-management)
- **Decision Power:** Low-Medium - recommends, doesn't usually decide on major purchases
- **Scope:** Individual work area or project leadership
- **Budget Authority:** Minimal or project budgets only
- **Best For:** Technical decisions, product feedback, user research (usually NOT major purchasing)
- **Common Titles:** Senior [Role], Senior Engineer, Senior Analyst, Senior Specialist

---

### **Intern**
- **Definition:** Intern position (temporary, learning-focused)
- **Decision Power:** None - learning role
- **Scope:** Individual tasks under supervision
- **Budget Authority:** None
- **Best For:** Usually NOT for B2B outreach or purchasing decisions

---

### **Entry**
- **Definition:** Entry-level position
- **Decision Power:** None - learning role
- **Scope:** Individual tasks under supervision
- **Budget Authority:** None
- **Best For:** Usually NOT for B2B outreach or purchasing decisions

---

## Recommended Combinations

### **For High-Value Leads (Decision Makers):**
```
seniority=Founder/Owner,C-Suite,Vice President
```
**Best for:** Strategic partnerships, large purchases, enterprise sales

---

### **For Department-Level Leads:**
```
seniority=Head,Director,Manager
```
**Best for:** Department-level decisions, vendor selection, operational purchases

---

### **For All Decision Makers:**
```
seniority=Founder/Owner,C-Suite,Partner,Vice President,Head,Director
```
**Best for:** Comprehensive decision-maker targeting

---

### **For Strategic + Operational:**
```
seniority=Founder/Owner,C-Suite,Vice President,Head,Director,Manager
```
**Best for:** Full spectrum of decision-making roles

---

## What to Avoid

### **Usually NOT for B2B Purchasing:**
```
seniority=Senior,Intern,Entry
```
**Why:** These roles typically don't have purchasing authority

**Exception:** Senior roles might influence technical decisions, but usually don't approve purchases

## Example Commands

### Example 1: Target Founders/Executives Only
```
/lead-magnet keywords=golf retailers | industry=Retail | seniority=Founder/Owner,C-Suite
```

### Example 2: Target All Decision Makers
```
/lead-magnet keywords=tech resellers | industry=Technology | seniority=Founder/Owner,C-Suite,Partner,Vice President,Head,Director
```

### Example 3: Target Department Leaders
```
/lead-magnet keywords=outdoor gear | industry=Retail | seniority=Head,Director,Manager
```

### Example 4: Exclude Entry-Level
```
/lead-magnet keywords=athletic wear | seniority=Founder/Owner,C-Suite,Partner,Vice President,Head,Director,Manager,Senior
```
(Excludes Intern and Entry)

## Important Notes

### Formatting
- Use exact values as shown: `Founder/Owner` (not "Founder" or "Owner" separately)
- Use `/` (slash) for combined values
- Case-sensitive - use exact capitalization

### Multiple Values
- Separate with commas: `seniority=Founder/Owner,C-Suite,Vice President`
- No spaces around commas: ✅ `C-Suite,Vice President` ❌ `C-Suite, Vice President`
